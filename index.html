Our Story

The Sankofa Seed Vault was born from a simple yet profound realization: our traditional seeds are disappearing, and with them, generations of cultural knowledge and food sovereignty.

"Sankofa" is a word from the Akan people of Ghana. It means "go back and fetch it" - a reminder that we must reach back to our roots to move forward wisely. This principle guides everything we do.

For too long, our communities have faced pressure to adopt standardized, corporate, or government-owned seeds that disconnect us from our heritage. Traditional seeds carry more than genes - they carry stories, ceremonies, recipes, and the wisdom of our ancestors.

Cultural Heritage

Every seed has a story. We preserve not just the seeds, but the cultural knowledge and traditions that make them meaningful.

Food Sovereignty

Communities should control their own food systems. Traditional seeds give us independence from external pressures.

Community Trust

Our Seed Keeper verification system ensures authenticity and builds a network resistant to corporate infiltration.

Resilience

Traditional seeds are adapted to local conditions and resistant to many challenges that affect standardized varieties.
